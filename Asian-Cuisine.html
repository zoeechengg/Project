Asian Cuisine

Asian cuisine includes several major regions, where many cultures have their own unique characteristic cuisine. Of the many different cuisines that come from the continent, a few of the main cuisines are - Japanese, Indian and Vietnamese. There are also many restaurants that offer Asian fusion as well, which is quite popular.

Japanese

Yakiniku Great

Location: 255 Queen's Road Central, Sheung Wan, Hong Kong

"Yakiniku" stands for grilled meat in Japanese and this is exactly what this restaurant does best. With grills in the center of each table, Yakiniku Great's focus is on its meats. They offer different cuts of the cow, cooked at specific times and paired with different sauces for each cut. It is clear how much effort and thought is put into each slice of meat served at Yakiniku Great, and the servers can either cook them for you or you can do it yourself, which in my opinion is a lot more fun! The wagyu is so soft that it melts in your mouth the second you eat it.


Porker

Location: B/F, 55 Wellington St, Central, Hong Kong (Entrance on Pottinger St)

One of my favorite Japanese dishes is "tonkatsu" which is a breaded, deep-fried/tempura pork cutlet. Here at Porker, their entire menu focuses on just that. They are a specialty Japanese cutlet restaurant, serving different types of meats and seafood made tonkatsu style.


Zuma

Location: Landmark Level 5 & 6, 15 Queen's Road, Central, Hong Kong

Located in the central business district of Hong Kong and at one of the most prestigious addresses in Central, Zuma Hong Kong is an internationally well known contemporary Japanese Izakaya restaurant with locations in New York, Boston, Miami, London, Dubai, just to name a few.

With their very own garden terrace, private room and a lounge and bar, Zuma's relaxing atmopshere elevates the dining experience. My recommendation is to go on the weekend when they have a brunch special with a sushi/sashimi and a hot foods buffet, free flow of champagne (additional charge), a choice of one entree and their famous dessert platter.


Kappo ro ann

Location: Shop 2801, 28/F, Isquare, 63 Nathan Road, Tsim Sha Tsui, Hong Kong

Focused on ingredients sourced from Kumamoto in Japan, Kappo ro ann offers fresh sushi and a new take on classic Japanese style cooked dishes. They are well known for their large chicken wings and raw chicken dish. In addition, Ro ann's restaurant offers sushi bar seating where sushi chefs provide meal goers with an unforgettable experience with their omakase set. The restaurant is also situated on the 28/F of iSquare (in Tsim Sha Tsui), overlooking the Victoria harbor.


Tensho

Location: 2/F Henry House, 42 Yun Ping Rd, Causeway Bay, Hong Kong

For the price of Tensho, the quality of the food you get is unbeatable. Located in Causeway Bay, tucked away in small building, Tensho is another great Japanese restaurant offering anything between sushi and sashimi to izakaya dishes as well. It is definitely a solid restaurant to dine at when you want something easy and casual.



Indian

Bombay Dreams


Source: https://hk.asiatatler.com/restaurants/bombay-dreams

Location: 4/F, Carfield Building, 77 Wyndham Street, Hong Kong

Located in the busy party district of Hong Kong, Bombay Dreams offers classic Indian dishes with a contemporary twist to it. Their portions are large and meant for sharing with friends and families. Created by Indian chefs, you can definitely taste the authenticity in their foods, more specifically their fragrant desserts. They also offer classic cocktails that incorporate Indian spices and serve, in my opinion, one of the best garlic naan I've had.



Vietnamese

Brass Spoon


Source: https://tinyurbankitchen.com/brass-spoon-hong-kong-pho/

Location: G/F, 10 Pottinger Street, Central, Hong Kong
Shop B, G/F, 1-3 Moon Street, Wan Chai, Hong Kong

One of the more popular Vietnamese Pho restaurants in Hong Kong is Brass Spoon, which has earned the Michelin's Bib Gourmand status, given to restaurants who have "exceptionally good food at moderate prices". Although compared to a lot of the other chain Pho restaurants in Hong Kong, Brass Spoon is considered a little on the pricer side, they pride themselves in offering premium ingredients like "preimum raw beef". One must fill in and tick a little sheet of paper to customize the ingredients and toppings added into your pho. I love the fact that you are allowed to add more or take away certain ingredients as you please when you order the noodles and their use of quality ingredients.



Asian Fusion

Glasshouse

Location: Shop 4009, 1 Harbour View Street, Central, Hong Kong

Compared to the rest of the rooftop restaurants and bars in the shopping mall of IFC, Glasshouse offers great prices for its Asian Fusion dishes. With amazing views of the harbor in the restaurant's literal glass house structure, Glasshouse is a perfect spot for casual dinner with friends and family. Their menu boasts of a huge variety of dishes from mains to pastas, soup noodles, robata, even rice dishes as well.

My personal favorite dish at Glasshouse is their duck noodle soup. Not only is the shredded duck meat juicy, but the noodle's broth is extremely flavorful, making this a great dish for a cold winter day.
